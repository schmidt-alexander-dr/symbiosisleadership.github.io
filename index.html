Solving coordination problems in climate and sustainability

Independent advisory working at the intersection of carbon accounting, supply chain traceability, climate finance, and standards governance — bridging disciplines, institutions, and sectors.

Practice

Working in the middle

Many of the hardest problems in sustainability aren't technical — they're coordination failures between disciplines, organisations, and standards bodies that don't naturally talk to each other. Symbiosis Leadership works in that gap.

Dr. Alexander Schmidt brings a PhD in Econometrics and over a decade of applied experience to engagements that span strategic advisory, ecosystem integration, and governance design.

Carbon Accounting

Emissions measurement, reporting frameworks, and data infrastructure for credible climate disclosure.

Supply Chain ESG

Traceability architecture, due diligence systems, and interoperability across value chains.

Standards Governance

Active contribution to international standards development through UN bodies and industry coalitions.

Climate Finance

Strategic advisory connecting climate-relevant ventures with financing pathways and investor narratives.

Approach

Engagements

Work typically begins with a focused exploration — a short, bounded engagement to map the problem and establish fit. From there, advisory can continue as retained support, project-based sprints, or on-demand consultation.

Current affiliations include the UNECE Team of Specialists on ESG Traceability, the UN/CEFACT Supply Chain Working Group, the PACT Network, the Carbon Accounting Alliance, and the Exponential Roadmap Initiative.
