Symbiotic Leadership

The change I seek to make

For decades, we've treated economic growth and planetary health as separate ledgers. Buy now, pay later. But the bill is arriving — in disrupted supply chains, volatile energy markets, extreme weather, and a generation of leaders who sense that something fundamental needs to shift.

Symbiotic Leadership means recognizing that none of us will be well if the planet is not. It's a shift from extractive to generative — in how we think, lead, and create value.

This isn't a call to renounce ambition. It's a return to the original meaning of competition — com-petere, to strive together. The change starts ground-up, with leaders who have the humility to admit they don't have all the answers alone — and the courage to find them together.

How I work

Spaces for leaders to recalibrate

Symbiotic Leadership is learned in practice, not in theory. I create spaces where leaders step out of their day-to-day, reconnect with what matters, and develop the integrated skillset that these times demand — together with others who share that commitment.

1:1 Coaching

Deep, individual work for leaders navigating the shift — from clarifying your theory of change to developing an integrated response to the interconnected challenges your organization faces.

Teams & Projects

Structured sprints with leadership teams tackling their specific challenges. Building the cross-cutting skillset of Symbiotic Leadership together: rethinking finance, supply chains, carbon, and governance as one interconnected system.

Retreats

In-person gatherings that bring back physical and emotional connection among leaders. Space to breathe, share challenges openly, gain perspective, and build a community of committed action.

Background

Built at the intersections

The Symbiotic Leadership philosophy grows from over a decade of applied work at the intersection of carbon accounting, supply chain ESG traceability, climate finance, and standards governance. I hold a PhD in Econometrics and have worked as an independent advisor bridging disciplines, institutions, and sectors — the kind of coordination problems that siloed expertise alone cannot solve.

This cross-cutting experience informs everything I bring to leaders today: a deep understanding of the four fundamental challenges and a practical, integrated approach to responding to them.
